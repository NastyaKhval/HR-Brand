01
Month 1: Cultural Code
Building the HR brand foundation

Goal of the Month

Form the company’s Cultural Code

CEO interview, candidate persona, EVP formulations, Story Bank — everything that will serve as the foundation for content and positioning.

1.1 Deep-Dive CEO Interview

The key source of the cultural code. Format: 60–90 minutes, recorded interview.

About the Founding

  • Why did you start Alconost?
  • What did you want to do differently?
  • How did you choose the teal model?

About the Culture

  • What kind of person thrives here?
  • What qualities are unacceptable?
  • How do you tell “our person / not our person”?

About Ambitions

  • Where is Alconost heading in 3 years?
  • What inspires you today?
  • What challenges lie ahead?

About the People

  • Who on the team do you consider a role model?
  • Is there an “Alconost type” of person?
  • What do employees say unofficially?

Output: transcript → key quotes → foundation for EVP, stories, and content.

1.2 Ideal Candidate Profile (Candidate Persona)

A document defining “who we’re looking for” — not by hard skills, but by mindset and cultural fit.

Parameter Description
Mindset Autonomous, entrepreneurial, comfortable with ambiguity
Motivation Wants to build, not maintain. Wants impact, not stability
Cultural Fit Values freedom, ready for responsibility. Not looking for a corporate ladder
Red Flags Needs constant supervision, seeks a “prestigious brand name”, not ready for async communication
Where They Live LinkedIn, startup communities, remote-work platforms, tech meetups

1.3 EVP — Employee Value Proposition

Key formulations of the value of working at Alconost, organized into 4 pillars:

Impact & Ownership

“You’re not joining a team — you’re building the market. Your deals = company growth.”

Freedom & Trust

“Remote. Flexible schedule. Teal structure. We hire adults and treat them like adults.”

Growth & Learning

“AI tools, cross-functional experience, international environment. You grow faster than in a corporation.”

Global Community

“20+ countries on the team. English every day. Multicultural experience is our norm.”

1.4 Story Bank

Collected real employee stories — the foundation for all future content.

Sources

  • CEO interview
  • Mini-interviews with 3–5 employees
  • Quotes from Slack / internal channels
  • Onboarding stories

Story Formats

  • “Why I chose Alconost”
  • “My typical day”
  • “What surprised me”
  • “My growth in N months”

Usage

  • LinkedIn posts
  • Careers page
  • Podcast episodes
  • Job descriptions

Month 1 Deliverables

  • CEO interview transcript + key quotes extracted
  • Ideal Candidate Profile document
  • EVP formulations (4 pillars)
  • Story Bank (minimum 10 stories/quotes)
  • Company Cultural Code (1-page summary)

Month 2: Internal Launch
Culture Talks podcast, social media, Glassdoor

Goal of the Month

Launch Culture Talks podcast + social media

Internal podcast (4 episodes), social media toolkit for employees, first Glassdoor reviews. From foundation — to content.

Culture Talks — Internal Podcast

Format: audio/video 15–20 minutes. 4 episodes per month. Each with a specific focus.

Can be recorded via Zoom/Riverside — minimal production overhead.

01

CEO: Why Alconost Is Built the Way It Is

The founder talks about the teal model, company history, and management philosophy. Best moments from the Month 1 interview + additional questions.

02

Employee: How to Work Remotely Without Losing Your Mind

Remote-work hacks, routines, balance. Real experience firsthand. Showing that remote work is a conscious choice, not a forced measure.

03

Team: What Teal Means in Practice

2–3 people discuss how decisions are made, what “no boss” really means, and the challenges and advantages of self-management.

04

Newcomer: First 90 Days at Alconost

An onboarding story. What surprised them, what was hard, what helped. The episode you can show to candidates.

Social Media Toolkit for Employees

A set of materials so employees can easily share content on their social media:

What’s Included

  • LinkedIn post templates
  • Ready-made company descriptions
  • Visual templates (Canva)
  • Guide: “How to talk about your work”

How to Motivate

  • Lead by example from CEO and HR
  • Support: “We’ll help you write it”
  • Internal recognition of active participants
  • No pressure — only inspiration

Glassdoor: First Reviews

Ask 3–5 loyal employees to leave honest reviews on Glassdoor. Not scripted — just suggested topics:

  • Culture and values
  • Work-life balance
  • Career growth and learning
  • What they like most
  • What can be improved (shows honesty)

Month 2 Deliverables

  • 4 Culture Talks podcast episodes (recorded + published)
  • Social Media Toolkit for employees
  • 3–5 Glassdoor reviews
  • First employee posts on LinkedIn
  • Internal communication about HR brand

Month 3: Vacancies & Scale
Rewrite vacancies, Talent Pool, external publications

Goal of the Month

Rewrite all vacancies + go external

Vacancies with the new EVP, Talent Pool formation, first external publications, metrics collection. Turning the foundation into a working machine.

3.1 Rewrite All Vacancies

Every vacancy must communicate the cultural code and EVP, not just be a dry list of requirements.

Element Before After
Title Account Executive Account Executive — Build the Korean Market from Scratch
Introduction We are looking for... Story: why this role matters, what impact, what you’ll build
About Us Alconost is a localization... Culture block: teal, remote, 120+ languages, real employee quotes
Requirements 3+ years experience... Mindset + skills. “You’re a fit if...”
Closing Send CV to... CTA with value proposition: “Ready to build a market? Here’s how the process works...”

10 High-Impact Article Ideas

Each article is designed to attract the right candidates by showing how we actually work. Every piece doubles as a cultural filter.

ARTICLE 1

“How We Manage 1,000+ Freelance Linguists Remotely (Without Losing Our Minds)”

Author: Head of Vendor Management

Problem: Managing 1,000+ freelancers across time zones with no office, no traditional KPIs, and complete autonomy — how does this even work?

Cultural Message: We hire self-organized people who don’t fear chaos, can build their own processes, and don’t wait for hand-holding.

ARTICLE 2

“We Don’t Use KPIs for Project Managers (And Our Clients Are Still Happy)”

Author: Head of Project Management

Problem: How do you ensure quality and client satisfaction without rigid KPIs, dashboards, or micromanagement metrics?

Cultural Message: We give freedom but expect ownership. If you need strict KPIs and control, this isn’t for you.

ARTICLE 3

“Stagility: How We Balance Startup Speed with Corporate Stability”

Author: CEO or COO / Head of Operations

Problem: Startups = chaos, corporations = stagnation. How do you find the balance in a remote team?

Cultural Message: We’re not for people who want complete certainty or constant adrenaline. We’re for those who can work in the grey zone.

ARTICLE 4

“Building an 83-Person Team Across [X] Countries Without an Office: What We Learned”

Author: CEO or Head of People

Problem: How do you build and maintain culture, communication, and connection when your team has never been in the same room?

Cultural Message: We hire people who can build their own connections, don’t wait for top-down initiatives, and thrive in autonomy.

ARTICLE 5

“When Everything Went Wrong: How Our Team Saved a Project Without Escalating Up”

Author: Senior PM or Team Lead

Problem: Major client, tight deadline, 3 freelancers drop out last minute — classic disaster scenario.

Cultural Message: We hire people who take ownership and don’t wait for someone else to solve problems for them.

ARTICLE 6

“How We Onboard Freelancers in 48 Hours (With No Hand-Holding)”

Author: Head of Vendor Management

Problem: How do you get freelancers productive fast in a remote, autonomous environment without micromanaging their first weeks?

Cultural Message: We hire people who hit the ground running — proactive, self-sufficient, and don’t need constant guidance.

ARTICLE 7

“Why We Choose Long-Term Relationships Over Fast Hires (Even When It Hurts)”

Author: CEO or Head of People

Problem: In a fast-paced industry, why do we sometimes leave positions open for months instead of just hiring someone “good enough”?

Cultural Message: We’re building for the long term, not just filling seats. We’d rather wait for the right person than compromise on culture.

ARTICLE 8

“What Happens When You Give Remote Teams Full Autonomy (A 3-Year Experiment)”

Author: CEO or Head of Operations

Problem: What actually happens when you remove traditional management controls in a remote company? Does it work or fall apart?

Cultural Message: Autonomy works when you hire adults who want ownership. If you need to be managed, we’re not a fit.

ARTICLE 9

“How to Build Processes That Don’t Kill Flexibility (Lessons from Managing Localization at Scale)”

Author: Head of Operations / Senior PM

Problem: How do you scale processes without turning into a bureaucratic nightmare that slows everything down?

Cultural Message: We build processes to enable people, not control them. We trust our team to know when to follow the process and when to break it.

ARTICLE 10

“The Real Cost of Remote Work (That Nobody Talks About)”

Author: CEO or Head of People

Problem: Everyone talks about remote work benefits, but what are the real challenges and costs that companies don’t prepare for?

Cultural Message: Remote work isn’t for everyone — it requires self-discipline, proactivity, and comfort with isolation. We’re honest about this.
